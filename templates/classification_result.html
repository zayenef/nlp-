Traits Descriptions

Extraversion

Extraversion refers to an individual's preference for social interaction, enthusiasm, and assertiveness. People high in extraversion tend to be outgoing, talkative, and energized by social situations.

Neuroticism

Neuroticism represents the degree of emotional instability and negative affect experienced by an individual. People high in neuroticism may be prone to experiencing anxiety, worry, and mood swings.

Agreeableness

Agreeableness reflects an individual's tendency to be compassionate, cooperative, and considerate towards others. People high in agreeableness are empathetic, understanding, and value harmonious relationships.

Conscientiousness

Conscientiousness pertains to the degree of organization, responsibility, and self-discipline displayed by an individual. People high in conscientiousness are reliable, self-motivated, and diligent in their work.

Openness

Openness refers to an individual's inclination towards new experiences, creativity, and intellectual curiosity. People high in openness are imaginative, open-minded, and receptive to new ideas.
